The **visible identity set** is the boundary of what a consumer can see in the network. This is how ParaDigi Universe contains spam without a central moderator.

### Trust Transfer
Consumers do not need a universal directory of good identities. They can grow visibility through a local trust graph.

- If you trust Publisher A, and Publisher A positively interacts with Publisher B, your client may decide to include Publisher B.
- This can extend through multiple layers, though shallow expansion is usually enough.
- Blocking removes paths locally, so trust does not have to spread everywhere.

### Zero Global Consensus
There is no global trending page, global blocklist, or system-wide ranking of relevance.

- your visible identity set is **private** and **local**
- two consumers can have radically different versions of the network
- information quality emerges statistically from many independent filtering decisions
